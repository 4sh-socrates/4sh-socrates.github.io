Our Languages

Multilingualism can be a secret superpower.

Millions of bright vernacular-medium students fall behind — not for lack of talent, but for lack of quality education in the language they actually think in. India has 26 official languages. 57 % of its internet users consume content in regional languages. The civilisational depth of Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, and Marathi is centuries old. The failure to teach STEM in those languages is recent — and entirely fixable.

A language is not just words.
It is a way of seeing the world.
The Problem

One ladder. One language. Millions left behind.

India’s education system runs on a single flawed assumption: that a good teacher is always available. For the majority of India’s 265 million students — educated in regional languages, studying in government schools, frequently female — that assumption has never been true.

More than 13,000 students died by suicide in India in 2021 — more than farmer suicides. 6.5 million failed their board exams. An entire generation is being prepared for competitive examinations without the mental resilience or the language support to survive them. These are not accidents. They are structural outcomes of a system built for someone else.

The Student

Not a victim.
A protagonist.

Our system ignores the most essential part of education: mental strength. Young adults today lack resilience, self-awareness, and inner stability — not because they are weak, but because no one taught them how to build it.

The most capable students in India are invisible to the system. They study in the “wrong” language, in under-resourced schools, without mentors or roadmaps — and without the inner resilience that difficult, sustained learning demands. A capable, persistent student who is given the right method, the right language, and the right resilience tools does not need expensive institutional support. She compounds on her own.

What We’re Building

Quality teaching.
In their language.

4sh is a method — a designed system for self-directed STEM learning that does not require a human tutor. It is built for government school students across India’s regional languages. It rests on five interlocking pillars: mental resilience, vernacular learning, career intelligence, competitive exam preparation, and AI-powered self-mastery. Not five separate products. One integrated experience, deliberately sequenced.

I hold degrees from IIT Kharagpur, Indian School of Business, University of Tennessee and Harvard. I spent six years at McKinsey and have worked at Goldman Sachs, Bank of America, and Meta. When I turned to education, I brought the same rigour — because the students who need it most deserve nothing less. 4sh is not a startup. It is a long-term conviction. — Dr. Samudra Dasgupta, Founder
